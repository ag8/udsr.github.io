biofuel.

Sun’s radiant energy shines on the leaves of plants

Photosynthesis converts sunlight to chemical energy

The plant is burned, releasing thermal energy

The thermal energy is used to turn water into steam

The steam turns a turbine, causing mechanical energy

A generator transforms this mechanical energy into electrical energy

hydroelectric.

Water rushes through an opening, where it spins a turbine, forming mechanical energy

A generator transforms this mechanical energy into electrical energy

geothermal.

Thermal energy from the hotspots heat up reservoirs of water, releasing steam

The steam turns a turbine, causing mechanical energy

A generator transforms this mechanical energy into electrical energy

wind turbines.

Wind spins a turbine, causing mechanical energy

A generator transforms this mechanical energy into electrical energy

urinal energy.

Microbes in the fuel cell feast on the urine, transforming it into chemical energy

It creates a cathode and an anode side, causing electrical energy

solar hot water.

Sun’s thermal energy heats hydrogen tube, which heats water

Temperature difference between the hydrogen and the atmosphere run a Stirling engine, which transforms the energy into mechanical energy

A generator transforms this mechanical energy into electrical energy

tidal wave energy.

Oceanic waves cause water to rush in and out of a tube, where it spins turbines, causing mechanical energy

A generator transforms this mechanical energy into electrical energy

wind kites.

High-altitude winds spin a wind kite’s turbines, transforming this energy into mechanical energy

A generator transforms this mechanical energy into electrical energy
